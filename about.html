About Us

Nwaru Oluchi Caroline

Founder, Women Aid Empowerment Network Initiatives

Introduction

Nwaru Oluchi Caroline, the founder of Women Aid Empowerment Network Initiatives, has emerged as a compassionate and dedicated philantropist. Her journey rooted in her media career, has evolved into a mission of humanitarian efforts, community development, and social advocacy, driven by the pressing needs of women and the Girl Child in Nigeria and Africa today.

Early Beginnings

Oluchi's story began in radio, where she captivated audiences as a presenter for the Fanta Fun Time and kids program at Kidan radio in kano state. Her talent led her to diverse roles, including traffic reporting fand achoring student talk shows at Cross River State Broadcasting Corporation (CRBC) in Calabar.

Professional Growth and National Impact

During her National Youth Service Corps NYSC, Oluchi served with the Human Right Commission , which shaped her commitment to advocacy and public service. She gained national broadcasting experience with the national television authortiy (NTA) and excelled at leadership newspaper as an advert executive and reporter in Abuja. Her freelancing and work with Moon report magazine demonsrtrated her dedication to journalism.

Entrepreneural Ventures and Media Leadership

Her Entrepreneurial spirit led her to establish Tent Mag, an in-house magazine. Her role as a reporter with Atlantic TV Network (ATN) further solidified her media industry reputation.

Media and Personal Assistant Roles

Oluchi's exceptional skills earned her positions as the media aide to the personal assistant (PA) for the wife of the Governor of Osun State, Erelu Ngozi Abeni Adeleke, and political figure Senator Andy Ubah and Hon. Ben Etu. These roles highlighted her ability to manage high-profile media relations effectively.

Founding Women Aid Empowerment Network Initiatives

Drawing on her extensice experience, Oluchi founded the Women Aid Empowerment Network Initiatives to address the urgent needs of the less privileged Women and the Girl Child in Nigeria.
The foundation's accomplishments include:

  • Community Support Initiatives.
  • Providing food, clothing and shelter to individuals in need
  • Launching vocational training programs to improve self-sufficiency.
  • Empowering Girls through Education.
  • Conducting workshops on menstrual health management to break stigma.
    Distributing sanitary products to women and the girl child in undeserved communities.
  • Advocacy and Outreach programs
  • Organising health camps and free medical check-ups
  • Facilitating educational programs and literacy campaigns

Conclusion

Nwaru Caroline Oluchi's journey from media profession to philanthropist, showcases her unwavering dedication to malking a difference. Through the Women Aid Empowerment Network Iinitiatives (WAENI). She addresses critical needs by empowering women and the Girl Child in communities and schools thereby enriching lives. Her work is a testament to the impact of compassion and commitment in transforming women and the Girl Child in the society, one life at a time. As the foundation grows, it remains poised to inspire and support Womwn and the Girl Child in need, paving the way for a brigher future.
